Software engineering is an essential task necessary for the functionality of national and international services and societies.

Many different types of software require vastly different operations, mechanics and systems to implement them. And it is almost pointless to look for a way to solve every problem with just one type of program. Thus every system requires a specialized software engineering technique.

As technology and software has progressed many software and service failures have emerged as a result, and in the authors view these failures are largely due to two main reasons.

  1. Increasing demand: Newer, larger and more complex systems have become too large in scale and old software engineering methods cannot cope with them. Thus newer methods are needed to suffice.
  1. low expectations: Many companies are not strict and adamant on proper software engineering etiquette and methods. Thus suffer in the long term when their systems become too large or complex to handle.

1.1

Professional software engineering goes beyond simply making a functional program or application. It involves techniques that can support the programs specification, design and evolution, none of which are usually relevant for personal development activities.

Software is also not just the developed program. Software can also include all the associated documentation and configuration data that is required to make these programs operate correctly.

Software engineers are concerned with developing software products.

  1. Generic Products: These are software products that are made to be sold on the open market to any customer that is able to purchase it.
  1. Customized (bespoke) products: These are systems that are customized and commissioned by a customer to suit their individual need. A software constructor develops that software particularly for that customer.

An important difference between these types of software is that, in generic products,
the organization that develops the software controls the software specification. For cus
tom products, the specification is usually developed and controlled by the organization
that is buying the software. The software developers must work to that specification.

But slowly are products are being based on a generic mindset and being adapted to suit individual needs, the line between these two different types of products are being blurred.

What do we mean when we talk about Quality?

When we talk about the quality of professional software, we have to take into
account that the software is used and changed by people apart from its developers.
Quality is therefore not just concerned with what the software does. Rather, it has to
include the software’s behavior while it is executing and the structure and organization
of the system programs and associated documentation. This is reflected in so-called
quality or non-functional software attributes. Examples of these attributes are the soft
ware’s response time to a user query and the understandability of the program code.

So basically the quality is not just determined by how well the code works, it also depends on how understandable the code is, how versatile it is, the degree of expandability, mutability, etc.

Why is Software Engineering important?

Software engineering is important for two reasons:

  1. More and more, individuals and society rely on advanced software systems. We
    need to be able to produce
    reliable and trustworthy systems economically and
    quickly.
  1. It is usually cheaper, in the long run, to use software engineering methods and
    techniques for software systems rather than just write the programs as if it was a
    personal programming project. For most types of systems, the majority of costs
    are the costs of changing the software after it has gone into use.

The systematic approach that is used in software engineering is sometimes called
a
software process. A software process is a sequence of activities that leads to the
production of a software product. There are four fundamental activities that are common to all software processes. These activities are:

  1. Software specification, where customers and engineers define the software that
    is to be produced and the constraints on its operation.
  1. Software development, where the software is designed and programmed.
  1. Software validation, where the software is checked to ensure that it is what the
    customer requires.
  1. Software evolution, where the software is modified to reflect changing customer
    and market requirements

Software engineering is related to both computer science and systems engineering:

  1. Computer science is concerned with the theories and methods that underlie com
    puters and software systems, whereas software engineering is concerned with the
    practical problems of producing software. Some knowledge of computer science
    is essential for software engineers in the same way that some knowledge of
    physics is essential for electrical engineers. Computer science theory, however, is
    often most applicable to relatively small programs. Elegant theories of computer
    science cannot always be applied to large, complex problems that require a soft
    ware solution.
  1. System engineering is concerned with all aspects of the development and evolution of complex systems where software plays a major role. System engineering is therefore concerned with hardware development, policy and process
    design and system deployment, as well as software engineering. System engineers are involved in specifying the system, defining its overall architecture,
    and then integrating the different parts to create the finished system. They are
    less concerned with the engineering of the system components (hardware,
    software, etc.)

Notions of Software Engineering Ethics that are not bound by law but rather professionalism:

  1. Confidentiality You should normally respect the confidentiality of your employ
    ers or clients irrespective of whether or not a formal confidentiality agreement
    has been signed.
  1. Competence You should not misrepresent your level of competence. You should
    not knowingly accept work that is outside your competence.
  1. Intellectual property rights You should be aware of local laws governing the use
    of intellectual property such as patents and copyright. You should be careful to
    ensure that the intellectual property of employers and clients is protected.
  1. Computer misuse You should not use your technical skills to misuse other
    people’s computers. Computer misuse ranges from relatively trivial (game playing
    on an employer’s machine, say) to extremely serious (dissemination of viruses or
    other malware)

Ethics can be very tricky as it can impact not only your own life, but the life of others such as your employer and employee. But not matter what situation you are in always keep yourself safe and try to manage everything through a legal and ethical channel. Remember that everyone is a human and can make mistakes, or be pressured.
